Station 1: iRead Wall - Guided Tour

Launched in December 2018, this AI-powered iRead Wall is an innovative facility created to promote digital reading. It’s more than just a wall—it’s like a passionate reading guide powered by technology, offering three main functions: themed e-book promotions, QR code downloads, and fun facial recognition book recommendations.

Visitors can browse online reading lists on the touchscreen based on their interests or scan a QR code to instantly borrow e-books.

The most eye-catching feature is the "Photo Book Recommendation," where facial recognition technology suggests personalized books. On the launch day in 2018, then-President Wu Chih-Yang personally tried it and selected *Life Is Like a Storybook* as his winter vacation read, inspiring the library’s “One Book for One Person” reading campaign.

During the 2019 school anniversary, Chairman Douglas Hsu and guests visited the wall and experienced the photo book recommendation firsthand. Since then, the wall has become not only a gateway to digital reading but also a popular photo spot for students and visitors alike.

It’s not just a wall—it’s a bridge connecting knowledge and your heart.
