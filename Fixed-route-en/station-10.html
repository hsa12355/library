Station 10: Zen Garden - Karesansui

Tucked away in a tranquil corner lies a Zen garden composed of white gravel and weathered stones—our very own *karesansui*, or dry landscape garden.

Here, “mountains” are represented by rocks, while “water” is suggested through meticulously raked sand patterns that mimic the gentle flow of streams.

Its static elements offer a profound sense of peace and stillness. The garden was designed with one intention: to invite every library visitor to slow down, quiet their mind, and immerse themselves fully in the world of books.

In 2022, President Ching-Jung Liao filmed a video here, reflecting on the value of accumulating energy during one's university journey. He chose this garden as the setting—not only for its serenity, but because it offers a gentle reminder: true strength often comes from the quietest places.

So, when you feel overwhelmed or tired, take a seat here. Let your eyes drift away from screens, let your thoughts settle. In this space, the world of books and the world within you quietly meet.
